The short version

From building software to running the transformation.

I started as a software engineer in Silicon Valley — seven years building products and engineering teams across three continents.

Then I took over an industrial robotics company. Learned the hard way what works inside a real business — and what doesn’t survive contact with a factory floor.

I joined the executive committee of a €160M listed aerospace company, 1,200 people. As Chief Transformation Officer, I built the operating handbook, deployed a company-wide ontology, set up data governance and cloud migration, hired and led a team of twelve.

Hypsis is the platform I wish I had had — a sovereign AI companion that sits inside the company, speaks the language of operations, and makes the transformation team faster without adding headcount.

What I bring

Three commitments I make to every company I work with.

01

Your data stays yours

I design systems where nothing leaves the building. Your processes are not redrawn to fit a vendor’s template. The model runs on your premises, and the ontology describes your business as it actually is — not as a software template decided abroad.

02

The team keeps running without me

No vendor lock-in, no cloud dependency, no cancellation that breaks operations. I build so the platform belongs to the company that uses it. If I leave, the system keeps running — and the team can keep building on it.

03

Models you can audit

You can only trust a model if you understand how it was trained, on what data, with which biases. I work with open, auditable models — not black boxes. That is what makes a serious answer possible when the question gets serious.
