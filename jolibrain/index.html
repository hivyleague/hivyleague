The reality

Every organization must integrate AI.
Four constraints block adoption.

No amount of vendor marketing can wish these away. They are structural — and they require a structural answer.

🔒

Sensitive data

Industrial processes, patient records, defense programs, financial positions — this data cannot leave the building, let alone the country.

⚙

Complex, specific systems

No two factories, hospitals, or procurement chains work the same way. Generic SaaS doesn’t fit; customization by consultants creates dependency.

⚠

External dependency is a strategic risk

When the AI that runs your operations belongs to someone else — their cloud, their models, their pricing — you’ve traded one dependency for another.

⚖

Strict accountability obligations

GDPR, NIS2, DORA, defense classifications, contractual NDAs, fiduciary liability for AI decisions. Compliance cannot be delegated to a vendor’s terms of service.

Our answer

Build and operate your own AI —
without external dependency, and fast.

How it works

From first contact to autonomous operations.

Week 1–2

Understand your operations

We map how your organization actually works — the decisions, the data flows, the friction points. Every detail and data matter. We re-formulate operational challenges as machine learning problems that best fit all constraints.

Week 2–4

Deploy the platform

The Hypsis Box arrives on-site — a physical server running AI inference, hosting apps, and storing your operational knowledge. One network cable, one IP address. IT provides nothing else. First apps go live.

Month 2–3

Build the tools alongside your teams

Your domain experts describe what they need in plain language. The AI Builder generates production applications. 3–10 apps go live: scheduling, quality control, procurement, maintenance. Your people build what they know best.

Day 90+

Autonomy

We hand over the platform, the knowledge base, and the skills. Internal teams keep building. The system improves because your people use it — not because a vendor pushes an update. The company becomes less dependent, not more.
